Toyota Camry

Launch: 1982

First Generation (1982-1986): The initial generation set the foundation for the Camry's reputation for reliability.
Second Generation (1987-1991): This generation saw a more significant presence in the North American market.
Third Generation (1992-1996): A more rounded and aerodynamic design was introduced.
Fourth Generation (1997-2001): Enhanced safety features and increased size characterized this generation.
Fifth Generation (2002-2006): More emphasis on performance and a sleeker design.
Sixth Generation (2007-2011): Introduction of a hybrid version and improved fuel efficiency.
Seventh Generation (2012-2017): Updated design and increased focus on technology and safety.
Eighth Generation (2018-present): Continued emphasis on technology, fuel efficiency, and a sportier design.

Ford Mustang

Launch: 1964

First Generation (1964-1973): The iconic debut model that defined the pony car segment.
Second Generation (1974-1978): A period marked by the impact of stricter emission standards and the oil crisis.
Third Generation (1979-1993): A return to a more performance-oriented focus.
Fourth Generation (1994-2004): A major redesign with a more modern and aerodynamic appearance.
Fifth Generation (2005-2014): A retro-inspired design with improved performance and handling.
Sixth Generation (2015-2020): Enhanced performance, updated design, and introduction of EcoBoost engine options.
Seventh Generation (2021-present): Continuation of the sixth generation with updates and improvements.

BMW 3 Series

Launch: 1975

Second Generation (1982-1994): Introduction of the popular "E30" series known for its handling and performance.
Third Generation (1990-2000): Expanded body styles and advanced safety features.
Fourth Generation (1998-2006): Introduction of the "E46" series with significant advancements in technology and performance.
Fifth Generation (2005-2013): Introduction of the "E90" series with improved handling and efficiency.
Sixth Generation (2012-2019): Introduction of the "F30" series with more advanced technology and a focus on fuel efficiency.
